Project name: RegolithFleet

Problem:
A team of robots must excavate a predefined dig site and transport removed regolith to a shared depot.

Research question:
How do uncoordinated, centralized, and decentralized coordination strategies affect excavation efficiency in a shared-depot multi-robot regolith haulage task?

Primary metric:
Makespan. ???

Secondary metrics:
Travel distance, congestion events, reroutes, idle time, utilization, loaded/unloaded travel time.

Environment:
Grid-based terrain with obstacles, excavation targets, and one depot.

Robot actions:
Move, dig, carry, dump, wait.

Algorithms to compare:
Greedy independent baseline, centralized planner, decentralized local-rule strategy.

Non-goals for v1:
Physics realism, Gazebo, sensors, continuous planning, detailed regolith modeling.
